47 Members of Village Savings Groups Benefit from Training

The women of Amuru are powerful forces for change and are amazingly determined and resourceful in their fight to achieve a better future. Over the years we have been working with the people of Amuru, we have found that by focusing our livelihood programme on women it has had a dramatic impact on the wellbeing of children, since they spend a significant proportion of their income on their children's food, health and education.

Last year, I am pleased to report that we had immense success with our ActionAid Village Savings Groups which had an average female participation of 85%. The savings groups are aimed at increasing overall household income through micro credit and savings schemes through targeting smallholder female farmers in local communities.

The farmer's savings group attend their bi-weekly meeting.

It's here that women save money and then borrow from those savings to meet their basic needs like their children's education, medical expenses and seeds. The best performing savings group last year was the "Pajaa Reflect Group" which has 48 members whom jointly saved an incredible 11,913,600 Shillings or â‚¬3,504. To strengthen engagement in this programme, we conducted agriculture training using Savings and Credit Cooperatives principles (SACCOs) and 47 leaders from 22 savings groups benefitted from this training in the districts of Amuru and Nwoya

After the success of the training, group leaders started to motivate even more groups to form an even bigger farmers' co-op and they now reach out across Amuru, Anaka and Lamogi sub counties in the districts of Amuru and Nwoya. They have been successful in rallying small holder farmers' and together they have positively influenced market prices through collective marketing and seed banking. With the strength gained by coming together, they have been able to reduce the power of middle men who constantly exploit disadvantaged small holder farmers' by offering them low prices for agricultural produce especially for groundnuts, sesame and maize.
