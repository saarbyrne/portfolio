One Hen Secures a Higher Education for a Young Girl!

“I am Agnes Lalam and I am 9 years old and I live in the village of Ayila with my father Obote Bosco and my mother Ajok Margret. I have 4 sisters and 4 brothers and I go to Gira primary school. I love school and this year I came sixth out of ninety three pupils in my class. My first hen was given to me in 2011 through a scheme run by PCD (Partner for Community Development) and ActionAid. I loved her so much and I used to feed her on maize and sorghum grains every day before leaving for school. After a year, I had 16 baby chicks and my hen was also laying eggs for my family," said Agnes Lalam.

"Between July and September 2012, I sold nine grown chickens each at 17,000 Shillings or €5 and made a profit of 153,000 Shillings or €45. With my Dad’s help, I used €17.60 of this money to buy a female piglet which grew and gave birth to 6 babies. Sadly, she became a little weak and was not feeding well for 2 weeks. At that time we didn’t have enough money to pay the vet, so my dad and I had to sell her at 119,340 shillings or €35.10. With this money, my dad was able to buy wholesale groundnuts which are cheap during the harvest season and is now trading them in Labongogali. While I’m looking after the 6 piglets, my dad is using the 119,340 shillings €35.10 in the market to trade. He told me that at the end of the trading season he will have enough money to be able to pay school fees for myself and my brothers and sisters," said Agnes Lalam.

"I thank my sponsor in Ireland and ActionAid for all the help my family has received. When the piglets grow up, I plan to sell them and buy a cow. This will really help my parents because we will be able to sell the milk it produces and in the future my education will be secure.” said Agnes Lalam.

They are not just animals, but friends who will support and finance my higher education.” Said Agnes Lalam (9)
